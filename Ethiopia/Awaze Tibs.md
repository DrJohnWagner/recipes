# Awaze Tibs

A slow-cooked Ethiopian beef stew built on berbere, caramelised onion and ghee. Traditionally served over injera.

**Prep Time:** 5 min | **Cook Time:** 40 min | **Total Time:** 55 min | **Servings:** 10

## Ingredients

* 6 Tbsp ghee or butter
* 3 cups yellow onion, finely chopped (about 2 large)
* 2 Tbsp ginger-garlic paste (1 inch ginger + 4 cloves garlic, crushed)
* 2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
* 6 Tbsp tomato paste
* 4 Tbsp berbere seasoning
* 2 Tbsp fried onions (optional — adds depth of colour)
* 4 tsp ground coriander
* 2 cups water, divided
* 1360 g beef stew meat, cut into 3 cm pieces
* 1 tsp sugar

## Steps

### Instant Pot

1. Set to sauté and heat for 30 seconds. Add ghee, then onion, ginger-garlic paste and salt. Cook for about 1 minute until slightly caramelised.
2. Add fried onions (if using), tomato paste, berbere, ground coriander and 1/2 cup water. Stir well and cook for 1 minute.
3. Add remaining 1 1/2 cups water and beef. Press cancel, seal the lid and pressure cook on Meat/Stew for 30 minutes. Natural release.
4. Taste and add sugar to balance the spices. If too thin, sauté uncovered for 3–5 minutes until the sauce tightens.

### Stovetop

1. Heat a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add ghee, then onion, ginger-garlic paste and salt. Cook for about 1 minute until slightly caramelised.
2. Add fried onions (if using), tomato paste, berbere, ground coriander and 1/2 cup water. Stir well and cook for 1 minute.
3. Add remaining 1 1/2 cups water and beef. Cover and cook for 35–45 minutes, stirring every 5–7 minutes, until fork-tender.
4. Uncover, taste and add sugar to balance. Simmer uncovered to desired thickness.

## Berbere Substitute

Combine and mix; use 2 Tbsp in the recipe above. Store the remainder in a sealed jar.

* 1 Tbsp paprika (or 2 tsp smoked paprika)
* 1 1/2 Tbsp coriander powder
* 1 Tbsp garam masala
* 2 tsp ground cumin
* 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
* 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
* 1/2 tsp turmeric
* 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg

## Notes

* If the beef is not fork-tender after 30 minutes in the Instant Pot, pressure cook for a further 5 minutes with natural release.
* Substitute beef stock for water to deepen the flavour.
* The sugar is not optional in practice — it resolves the spice balance.

## Tips

* Serve over Ethiopian injera. Basmati rice or couscous also work.
* For chicken (Doro Wot), pressure cook for 5 minutes. For lamb (Beg Wot), 20 minutes.
* Carrots and potatoes can be added with the meat for a heartier stew.
