# Thai Green Curry

**Prep Time:** 15 min | **Cook Time:** 15 min | **Total Time:** 30 min | **Servings:** 4

## Ingredients

### Protein

* 800 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 5–7 cm chunks

### Pressure phase vegetables

* 6–8 small Thai eggplants, halved (or quartered if large)

### Open phase vegetables

* 150 g green beans, trimmed and cut into 5–6 cm lengths
* 1 large red or yellow onion (≈200 g), sliced into thick petals
* 2 large mixed peppers — red, orange, or yellow (≈300 g), deseeded and cut into wide strips
* 100–150 g tinned bamboo shoots, drained and rinsed

### Aromatics

* 3–4 makrut (kaffir) lime leaves, torn once
* 20–30 g Thai basil leaves, picked (add at the end)
* 1–2 red chillies, thinly sliced

### Curry base

* 3–4 Tbsp green curry paste (≈60–80 g)
* 2 × 400 ml cans coconut milk (800 ml total)
* 3–4 Tbsp fish sauce, to taste
* 1–2 Tbsp palm sugar (15–30 g), shaved or chopped

## Steps

1. Set the Instant Pot to sauté. Add the coconut cream from the top of the first can and the curry paste. Stir constantly for 1–2 minutes until the paste is bubbling and fragrant.
2. Press cancel. Add the chicken, eggplant, lime leaves, remaining coconut milk, and half the fish sauce. Stir to combine.
3. Seal the lid and pressure cook on high for 4 minutes. Quick-release when done.
4. Set back to sauté. Add the onion, peppers, green beans, and bamboo shoots.
5. Cook for 3–5 minutes, stirring, until vegetables are crisp-tender. Do not overcook.
6. Add palm sugar and remaining fish sauce to taste. Adjust balance — the curry should be savoury, faintly sweet, and bright.
7. Stir in Thai basil and red chilli. Serve immediately over jasmine rice.

## Notes

* Pat chicken dry before cooking — better contact with the fat phase.
* Cut chicken into large, uneven chunks: too small and it overcooks under pressure.
* Slice onion into petals, not thin strips — it should soften without disappearing.
* Cut eggplant just before cooking to control oxidation.
* Tear lime leaves once to release oils; shredding early extracts bitterness.
* Add basil only at the end — it oxidises and dulls quickly once cut.
* Rinse tinned bamboo shoots thoroughly; blanch briefly if the smell is strong.

## Tips

* Keep coconut cream separated before opening the cans — use it to fry the paste for better flavour development.
* Wide pepper strips and consistent bean lengths give even doneness in the open phase.
* If the sauce is too thick, loosen with a splash of water. If too thin, simmer uncovered another 2 minutes.
