# Split Pea and Ham Soup

A thick, slow-built soup using Instant Pot chicken stock as its base, with split peas, lentils, black beans and ham hock.

## Ingredients

* 1 to 1 1/2 kg chicken bones
* 7 cups water
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp ground pepper
* 1 ham hock
* 1 large onion
* 3 medium carrots
* 3 stalks of celery
* 1 red capsicum
* 1 medium potato
* 3 or 4 cloves of garlic
* 1 cup dried split peas
* 1 cup dried lentils
* 1 cup dried black beans
* 4 cups boiling water
* 1 Tbsp olive oil
* 1 tsp sweet Hungarian paprika
* 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper

## Steps

1. Combine chicken bones, 7 cups water, salt and pepper in your 5.7L or 8L Instant Pot. Cook on high pressure for 2 hours.
2. While the stock cooks, finely dice onion, carrots, celery, capsicum and garlic into a large bowl. Shave the meat from the ham hock, dice and add to the bowl; set the hock aside.
3. Finely dice potato and cover with water in a small bowl to prevent browning.
4. Put black beans in a small pot, add 4 cups boiling water, and soak for 1 hour. Drain and set aside.
5. When the stock is done, drain through a strainer into a large bowl and set aside. Clean the Instant Pot.
6. Add olive oil and the diced vegetables to the Instant Pot. Sauté for 12 minutes, stirring regularly.
7. Add chicken stock, split peas, lentils, black beans, paprika and cayenne. Stir. Drain and add the potatoes and stir again.
8. Add the ham hock, submerging it as much as possible. Check that the soup is below the maximum volume line, removing some if necessary.
9. Cook on high pressure for 15 minutes. Let depressurise naturally.
10. Stir and serve.
