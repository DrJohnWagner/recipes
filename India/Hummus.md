# Instant Pot Hummus

**Prep Time:** 25 mins  
**Cook Time:** 35 mins  
**Total Time:** 1 hr  
**Servings:** 12 servings  
**Calories:** 106 kcal

Use your Instant Pot to make the best light and creamy hummus from dried beans in under an hour. Plus, this recipe requires no soak time!

## Ingredients

### BEANS:
* 1 pound dried garbanzo beans, rinsed
* 12 cups filtered water

### HUMMUS:
* 3 cups cooked garbanzo beans, still warm
* 1/2 cup warm bean cooking liquid
* 1/4 cup tahini
* 2 medium cloves garlic
* 1 large lemon, juiced
* 1 teaspoon kosher salt
* 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
* 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
* 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil, highest quality

## Steps

### TO COOK THE BEANS:
1. Rinse garbanzo beans and discard any stones. Place in Instant Pot insert along with 12 cups of filtered water.
1. Close lid, make sure vent is set to "sealed" and set Instant Pot to manual for 35 minutes.
1. Allow Instant Pot to naturally release pressure when done (this can take up to 1/2 hour). If you are pressed for time, allow IP to release pressure naturally for at least 15 minutes and then do a slow quick pressure release.
1. Carefully drain the beans, making sure to reserve the liquid.

### HUMMUS:
1. Transfer 3 cups of warm drained, cooked garbanzo beans to the bowl of food processor fitted with the chopping blade. Add all other ingredients EXCEPT olive oil.
1. Process until smooth and slowly add the olive oil through the tube, 1 tablespoon at a time.
1. Hummus should be smooth, creamy and taste almost whipped.
1. Serve topped with Za'atar, smoked paprika and a splash of olive oil and enjoy!

## Notes
* If you want to soak the garbanzo beans before cooking, reduce the cook time to 25 minutes under high pressure.
* The cook time is intended to cook the beans until they are slightly mushy. If you want to prepare cooked beans to use for a recipe other than hummus, reduce the cook time by 5 or 10 minutes.
* 1 pound of dry garbanzo beans yields about 9 cups of cooked garbanzo beans. This recipe intends to make extra beans. Portion off 3 cups plus 1/2 cup of bean liquid in glass containers and freeze to make additional batches of hummus. Slightly warm the beans and liquid before processing for hummus so it's nice and light.
* If you want to use canned beans for this hummus, use two cans of garbanzo beans, drained and substitute filtered water for the bean cooking liquid.
* Store prepared hummus in a sealed container in the fridge for up to 5 days.
